Editorial — The Case for This Correspondent
Section II.
“The rare data scientist who speaks both languages — business and data — without losing fluency in either.”
I.
Business In, Data Out

I can sit in a room where a business problem is being defined and leave knowing exactly what data is needed, what question to ask of it, and what the answer needs to look like for someone to act on it. Strategy doesn't get lost in translation.

II.
Data In, Decisions Out

I don't ship models — I ship clarity. The output of my work is always a decision someone can make with more confidence than before. I take results that only make sense inside a notebook and turn them into something a stakeholder can use.

III.
Curious by Default

I built an MCU plot generator because I wanted to understand how it worked — not because anyone asked. That kind of curiosity is the thing no job description can hire for directly, but every good team runs on.
